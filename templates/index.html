Registers

Registers are lists of information. Each register is the most reliable list of its kind.

Read an introduction to registers on GOV.UK.

How registers work

Registers are minimum viable datasets. They are a better way of storing simple lists of things in standardised ways, separate from technology platforms and digital services.

They have clear user needs and purpose. They share certain characteristics and are linked together.

Every register is looked after by a custodian. That person is responsible for ensuring that data is accessible, accurate, up-to-date, and hasn’t been tampered with.

In practice, not all datasets need to be registers.

Registers are a cross-government need. GDS wants to support departments and agencies while they build their registers for their own use and that of others in government. We’re looking forward to working with Digital Leaders and the Data Leaders Network to build this ecosystem of registers.
