Authoritative, factual data

A register is an official collection of factual data services need to meet user needs.

Services are verbs and registers are the nouns they act upon. Example registers include: address, clinical-commissioning-group, company, charity, country, mot-test, postcode, food-hygiene-rating, g-cloud-supplier, g-cloud-supplier-contact, hospital, post-town, public-body, register-office, school, and vat-rate.

The users of registers

Digital teams

Building services for users.

A straightforward API and standardised data means we can quickly build services in the context of government, and not just a single organisation.

Registrars

Custodians of one or more registers.

Registers improve the integrity of the data you hold, and simplify how data is managed. Registers plug into commodity work-management and caseworking systems.

Everyone

Citizens of a digital state.

Anyone can use open register data, and use registers to check the authenticity of data. Private data is kept private.

Improving the integrity of data

  • Authoritative

    A register has a clear definition, purpose, and is the official source of truth for a dataset.

  • Trustworthy

    Each register is owned and run by a registrar. Responsibility for managing the data held in a register is made clear, providing greater transparency and a clear process for redress.

  • Canonical

    Where data from one register relies on data in another register, the data is linked rather than duplicated, minimising redundancy and clarifying provenance.

  • Standardised

    Consistently named fields, constrained using standard datatypes and exposed through a uniform API helps reduce duplication, fraud and error.

  • Tamper-proof history

    Register entries are immutable, simplifying downloading, caching and copying of data. Individual entries are superseded rather than edited meaning the full history of changes to a register is preserved.

  • Independently verifiable

    A secure audit trail records any changes made to a register. The contents of a register may be independently monitored, and the contents checked using using publicly available tests.

  • Provable authenticity

    Physical-digital proofs allow users to independently prove the veracity of an entry in a registry. A register entry such as a certificate, permit or licence may later be retracted by a register, but cannot easily be refuted.

Designed for privacy

This alpha only uses freely available open data, along with some fictional test data.

During beta, some personal and other sensitive data may be held in a private register, but only with the explicit consent of users of alpha services.

Data stored in a private register is held safely and securely. It is never shared, and may only be accessed through authorised actions by trusted services in answer to specific questions about individual entries.
