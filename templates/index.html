Improving registers

Registers collect factual data the government needs to provide services. You’ve probably come across many types of registers, eg. Companies House holds a register of all UK businesses.

At the Government Digital Service, we’re creating an exemplar of how public registers of information should be held, shared and maintained. Improving registers will ensure public data can always be trusted.

See registers made to date

This alpha only uses freely available open data, along with some fictional test data. We’re not currently looking at private registers, which are held safely and securely, and only accessed through authorised actions.

Establishing standards

The improved registers will have a number of specific attributes

Distributed

Each register resides under its own registrar (e.g. a department or agency), which is fully accountable for the data they collect and maintain

Canonical

Registers are the single source of truth for platforms and services, with no duplicated data residing elsewhere

Verifiable data

Individuals can verify their licences, permissions or certificates, and service teams can check register data with publicly available tests

Long lived

Registers remain regardless of whether services change or retire

Tamper-proof

Registers have secure audit trails that can’t be edited, recording any changes made to the data

Responding to user needs

A number of user groups will benefit from the improved registers

Service teams

Registers with a standardised data model and straightforward APIs will help you build services quickly.

Registrars

Teams that are custodians of one or more registers will be able to trust the data they collect and maintain, and be assured it hasn’t been tampered with.

Citizens

Citizens will know which departments or agencies maintain and update their data, and which services have access to their data.
