Registers

Operational data for the UK government

What are registers?

A register is a collection of factual data that government needs in order to provide services, which meets a user need and that may be cited as the official source of truth. Example registers include: address, country, postcode, post-town, and school.

A complete list of registers is maintained in the register of registers.

Who are registers for?

Developers

Developers building services within or outside of Government.

A consistent, straightforward API with a standardised data model enable you to build services quickly.

See demonstrations built with registers here.

Registrars

Teams that are custodians of one or more registers.

Registers help guarantee the accuracy and validity of the data you use. Privileged services can extend registers directly into commodity work-management and caseworking systems.

Everyone

Citizens of a digital state.

Anyone can check open register data. Only the data absolutely essential to providing a service is stored in a register. Private data is protected, accessible only by attributes and critically, not shared.

The benefits of registers

  • Authoritative

    A register is the single, canonical source of truth for a dataset.

  • Trustworthy

    Each register is maintained by an authorised registrar. Ownership and responsibility for the data is made clear, providing greater transparency and a clear process for redress.

  • Linked data

    Where data from one register relies on data in another register, the data is linked rather than duplicated, minimising redundancy and reducing errors.

  • Standardised

    Consistently named fields using standard datatypes, behind a uniform API simplifies the building of platforms and services using data held in registers.

  • Independently verifiable

    Physical-digital proofs allow users to independently validate the authenticity of an entry in a registry.

  • Tamper-proof

    A secure audit trail records any changes made to a register. Individual entries are superseded rather than edited. The full history of a register is preserved.

    The contents of a register may be independently monitored, and the contents checked using using domain-specific tests.

  • Privacy by design

    Private and sensitive data is held separately, only accessible to authorised services in answer to specific questions about individual entries.

Registers, fields and datatypes

Each register consists of a set of fields such as: name, text, postcode, longitude, latitude, start-date and end-date. The values that may appear in a field are constrained by its datatype such as: string, integer, decimal-degrees, and curie.

A complete list of fields is maintained in the field register, and a complete list of datatypes in the datatype register.

This alpha is being developed as open source software by a small team at the Government Digital Service. We welcome contributions and feedback.
